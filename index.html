Is It Really Depression or Just a Bad Day?

It’s normal to feel down occasionally, but understanding the difference between depression and a temporary low can be essential for seeking the right help.

  • Signs of Clinical Depression: Persistent feelings of hopelessness, fatigue, loss of interest in usual activities, changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, and suicidal thoughts.
  • Temporary Feelings: Short-term sadness, mood swings, or stress caused by specific events, like exams or personal challenges.

If you’re unsure whether your feelings are signs of depression or temporary emotional distress, consulting a mental health professional can offer valuable insights.
